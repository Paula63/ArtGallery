Adventure into the Contemporary Impressionism

Contemporary Impressionism is a term used to describe works created by contemporary artists that share similar aesthetic qualities with those by the French Impressionists.

The museum of Copenhagen, Denmark will be holding a solo art exhibition for the multi talented artist known for his colourful works of art, Leonid Afremov. A vibrant showcase for the visual arts and a treasure trove of modern and contemporary art, welcomes back this wonderful collection. These superb works will be on exhibition for the enjoyment of all Danes and visitors to the country.

The masterpieces will be on view to the public on Monday, 15th April 2019. There will be a free public lecture on Afremov’s Little Bridge by Dr. Alex Cappock, Head of Collections at the Gallery on Sunday, 14th April 2019 at 3.00pm.

Leonid Afrémov is a Belarusian painter with Israeli nationality. His paintings usually refer to landscapes, urban scenes, flowers, seascapes and vividly colored portraits. He generally uses spatulas, as a tool, and oil painting, as a material, for his paintings.
